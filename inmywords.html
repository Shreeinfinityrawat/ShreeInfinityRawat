In My Words

Sheetal Rawat: I write to illuminate the quiet truths that often go unheard.

It has been five years since I first surrendered to the page — not to craft perfection, but to capture essence. What began as a quiet impulse has become an intimate dialogue with the world around me. I have always been more drawn to undercurrents than noise — to the nuanced gestures, fleeting glances, and fragile truths that lie beneath the surface of everyday existence. My work delves into the intricacies of human emotion — love in its layered forms, the architecture of relationships, the poetry of longing, and the silent evolution of self. I do not seek grandeur in expression; I seek honesty. What interests me is not how loudly a story can be told, but how deeply it can be felt. I have no desire to carve out a legacy built on recognition. If a single chapter I write allows one individual to pause, reflect, or rediscover a piece of themselves — then the purpose is fulfilled. Writing, for me, is both an offering and an observation — a way of distilling complexity into clarity, of finding grace within imperfection. My words do not demand attention; they extend an invitation. They whisper rather than shout, yet carry the weight of experience, empathy, and unwavering belief in quiet resilience. I am not here to impress, but to witness. Not to perform, but to connect. And if, through my words, someone finds light — however faint — then the pen has served its highest function.


– Sheetal Rawat
